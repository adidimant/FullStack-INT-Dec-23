Companies of vehicles and general information about them

As of 2022, there are around 20 car companies in the world Below is a breakdown of the companies and general information about them and the cars they offer


Toyota

Toyota was established in September 1933 as a division of the Toyoda company, which was engaged in the production of textile weaving machines. The first division manager was Kishiro Toyoda, son of Sakichi Toyoda, the company's founder. The division's first engine was developed in 1934, and the first cars (model A1 and G1) were introduced in 1935. In 1936, serial production of the first car, the AA model, began.[9] The automobile division became a separate company and was registered under the name 'Toyota' on August 28, 1937. That same year, the company's first automobile manufacturing plant was established in the city of Kuromo, Japan. During World War II the company was mainly engaged in the production of trucks for the Japanese army including the Su-Ki amphibious truck, and its factories were not bombed by the Allies. In 1947, production of the private cars, sold under the name "Toyopet" (Toyopet), resumed. The first model sold under this name was the SA. After entering the American market, it was decided to stop using this name, because it created an association for pets (pet in English). The name continued to be used in other markets until the mid-1960s. In 1956, the network of sales agents was established. In 1958, sales of the company's vehicles began in the United States, which at the end of the year amounted to the sale of 287 Crown vehicles as well as one Land Cruiser SUV.[10] In 1963, Toyota established its first factory outside of Japan in Melbourne, Australia.[11] In 1965 the company entered the field of sports cars and began sales of the "Toyota Sport 800".

Ford

The Ford Motor Company (in English: Ford Motor Company) is one of the largest international American car manufacturers in the world. In 2005, the Ford Group was in second place in the world car sales ranking, second only to General Motors, but starting in 2006, when Toyota overtook Ford in sales, it began to consistently fall in the ranking. In 2011 it was ranked as the fifth largest producer in the world. Ford's F model pickup truck series is the best-selling car in the United States every year, continuously since 1981. In 2004, approximately 940,000 units (a record) of this series were sold in the United States, most of them F-150 model. The Ford Group includes the Ford (United States and Europe) and Lincoln brands, and owns the British Aston Martin. In 2008, Ford sold its holdings in the British Land Rover and Jaguar to the Indian Tata. In 2010, Ford sold its holdings in the car division of the Swedish Volvo and the Japanese Mazda, and also stopped the production of the American Mercury brand.

Honda

Honda Motor Co., Ltd. (本田技研工業株式会社, Honda Giken Kōgyō Kabushiki gaisha, lit. 'Honda Institute of Technology and Industry Company', IPA: [honda] ⓘ; /ˈhɒndə/) is a Japanese public multinational conglomerate manufacturer of automobiles, motorcycles, and battery-powered equipment, headquartered in Minato, Tokyo, Japan. Honda has been the world's largest motorcycle manufacturer since 1959,[4][5] reaching a production of 400 million by the end of 2019.[6] It is also the world's largest manufacturer of internal combustion engines measured by volume, producing more than 14 million internal combustion engines each year.[7] Honda became the second-largest Japanese automobile manufacturer in 2001.[8][9] In 2015, Honda was the eighth largest automobile manufacturer in the world.[10] Honda was the first Japanese automobile manufacturer to release a dedicated luxury brand, Acura, in 1986. Aside from their core automobile and motorcycle businesses, Honda also manufactures garden equipment, marine engines, personal watercraft, power generators, and other products. Since 1986, Honda has been involved with artificial intelligence/robotics research and released their ASIMO robot in 2000. They have also ventured into aerospace with the establishment of GE Honda Aero Engines in 2004 and the Honda HA-420 HondaJet, which began production in 2012. Honda has two joint-ventures in China: Dongfeng Honda and GAC Honda. In 2013, Honda invested about 5.7% (US$6.8 billion) of its revenues into research and development.[11] Also in 2013, Honda became the first Japanese automaker to be a net exporter from the United States, exporting 108,705 Honda and Acura models, while importing only 88,357.[12]

Nissan

The company "Kwaishinsha Motorcar Works" from Tokyo, founded in 1911, built its first car three years later called DAT, after the initials of its founders. In 1925 the name of the company was changed to DAT. This company merged with Jitsuyo Jidosha, another car manufacturer from Osaka, and in 1931 the first Datsun car was produced. In 1932 Datsun type 11 was produced. The name DATSON was created from the combination DAT and Son meaning "son", but because "Son" means "loss" in Japanese, the name was changed to DATSUN. In 1933 the company's name was converted into Japanese as "Jidosha Seizo" and it moved to Yokohama. A year later it merged with another car manufacturer "Nihon-Sangyo" called "Ni-san" for short. This name became the company's official name in 1934. Similar to other Japanese manufacturers such as Isuzu and Hino Motors, Nissan partnered with a European car manufacturer to gain advanced design and manufacturing know-how. Nissan chose the English Austin company and began building in Japan the "Austin 7" and "Datsun 1000" models, which was based on the Austin A50. In the 1950s, Nissan began to introduce independently designed cars. In 1967, the 4-cylinder "L" engine was introduced, which was advanced for its time and had an overhead camshaft. This engine was assembled in the new sedan Datsun 510 which was quite successful in the local and export markets. In 1970, Nissan introduced the 240Z sports car model with a six-cylinder version of the "L" engine. The extroverted design and high capabilities of the 240Z elevated Nissan to the level of a major global car manufacturer. In 1966, Nissan merged with "Prince", another Japanese manufacturer, and gained more luxurious and larger car models such as "Skyline" and "Gloria". Nissan Model 70 Payton With the outbreak of the oil crisis of the seventies, Nissan was one of the largest car manufacturers in the world, as its cars were mostly economical and had a small engine. The company spread around the world and established factories in Australia, Mexico, Great Britain and several factories in the United States. The plant in Australia was closed in the late 1980s due to lack of economic justification, but another plant was opened in Pretoria, South Africa. During the 90s of the 20th century, Nissan deteriorated, its sales decreased, and the company reached the brink of liquidation. In 1999, the French Renault purchased controlling shares in Nissan and Carlos Ghosn was appointed as president. Gohan initiated the "Nissan Revival Program" (NRP) which was extraordinarily successful. Nissan returned to profitability and renewed the Nissan and Infiniti car line, which was a domestic American brand at the time. In 2001, Ghosen continued with the "Nissan 180" program for the continued growth of the company. His success in returning the company to profitability earned him great sympathy and publicity in Japan, almost to the point of national hero status, with many mentions in manga and popular culture. Emperor Akihito awarded him the Japan Medal with Blue Ribbon in 2004. One of Nissan's compact and luxury cars is the Nissan Maxima. In 2021, Nissan introduced the seventh car in the Z series, the Nissan Z.
