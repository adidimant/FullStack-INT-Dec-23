My Island

The northern lights

A natural phenomenon characterized by the appearance of light in different colors in the night sky. The phenomenon occurs near the North Pole, where it is called the Northern Lights

The glacier lagoon

In the southeast of Iceland you will find Jökulsárlón, a blue lake, filled with floating glaciers originating from the Vatnajökull glacier, the largest glacier in Europe.

Eyjafjallajokull volcano

The Eyjafjallajokull volcano and caldera is one of Iceland's smallest volcanoes, rising to a height of 1,651 meters. Despite this, it is one of the most active volcanoes in the country, and the one responsible for the most famous eruption in Iceland's history

Katla ice cave

This unique ice cave under the Katala volcano. Ride a superjeep and witness the breathtaking combination of fire and ice in one of the rarest natural phenomena.

Climbing the Sólheimajökull glacier

The Sólheimajökull glacier is a rugged Icelandic glacier full of impressive ice breaks, sometimes you also encounter waterfalls, ravines and you may even come across some natural ice caves.

Secret Lagoon

The lagoon was first discovered in 1891 at Hverahólmi, a geothermal area near the town of Fludir in the south. What makes it special are the rich natural resources of hot water that have been flowing from the ground for centuries: Vaðmálahver, Básahver or Litli Geysir are the natural sources of this lagoon.

Black beach Reynisfjara

Reynisfjara, the black beach in Iceland, is known as one of the most unique black beaches in the world. The reason for this is the combination of the basalt, the black sand, the giant cliffs that rise to a height of about 70 meters above the sea, and the Hálsanefshellir cave, the beautiful hexagonal cave on the coast.

Gýgjarfoss waterfall

Gýgjarfoss waterfall is one of the few waterfalls in the mountainous central region of Iceland. The waterfall is located on the Jökulfall river, which receives its water from the nearby Hofsjökull glacier and other rivers in the area.
